Hacking Innovation Confidence
A product manager can discover useful insights by walking in the customer's shoes. Through interviews, product trials, and ethnographic research, a product manager can discern what the customer really desires.
After distilling insights, it is incumbent on the product manager to effectively illustrate the ambition - to team members and eventually as a prototype to potential customers or partners. Coming up with creative lo-fi techniques to get the idea across is critical to keeping learning cycles short and opening viewers' minds to possibilities.
